Configuring TiddlyRemember

To access the Anki add-on settings, in Anki, choose Tools > Add-ons, then select TiddlyRemember and click the Config button. Here's an example configuration:

Global settings

The settings at the top apply to all wikis you might be syncing with.

Default deck
If a note in your TiddlyWiki doesn't match any of your deck mappings, it will be placed here.
Path to TiddlyWiki executable
TiddlyRemember requires TiddlyWiki on Node.js to be installed on your computer (see Installing TiddlyRemember). Depending on your computer's configuration, simply tiddlywiki may be sufficient here â€“ otherwise, you'll have to figure out where TiddlyWiki is installed on your computer and provide the full path (e.g., /usr/local/bin/tiddlywiki). Click the Test button to see if it works.

Wiki settings

You can add and remove any number of wikis to sync with. Each wiki has its own set of settings.

Name
This will appear on your Anki cards to identify the source of the information.
Type
This may be File, Folder, or URL.
  • File: A single-file wiki located on your computer.
  • Folder: A folder wiki located on your computer. Select the folder containing the tiddlywiki.info file.
  • URL: A single-file wiki hosted on the web somewhere, which can be downloaded without requiring authentication.
Path or URL
Either the path to the wiki (click Browse to look for it) or the URL of the wiki, depending on which option you chose for Type.
Filter
Only the tiddlers matching this TiddlyWiki filter will be searched for questions. It's important to make sure that tiddlers that can't be rendered to HTML (e.g., images) are excluded â€“ failing to do so may result in extremely long sync times or crashes. If you're not sure what to put here, just keep the default.
Permalink
A URL at which your wiki can be accessed on the devices where you review Anki cards. This will allow Anki to provide a link back to the source tiddler on your cards. This field is optional â€“ if you leave it blank, no link will be provided on your cards, but you'll still be able to see which wiki and tiddler it came from. If you use a file wiki on your local computer, you can use a file:// URL. For example, if you keep your wiki at /home/soren/tiddlywiki.html, you would enter file:///home/soren/tiddlywiki.html. You should be able to copy this URL out of the address bar of your browser.

Changes to the name or permalink will update all of the notes from that wiki on the next sync.
